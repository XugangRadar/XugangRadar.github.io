Biography

Gang Xu is currently Full Professor of southeast university, China. He received the Ph.D. degree in electronic engineering from Xidian University in 2015. From 2015 to 2016, he was a full-time Postdoctoral Research Fellow with the School of Electrical and Electronic Engineering (EEE), Nanyang Technological University (NTU), Singapore. From 2016 to 2022, he was an associate professor with the State Key Laboratory of Millimeter Waves, Southeast University. His current research interests are synthetic aperture radar (SAR), SAR interferometry (InSAR), inversed synthetic aperture radar (ISAR), sparse signal processing and microwave remote sensing. He has authored or coauthored more than 50 scientific journal articles and more than 30 papers in conference proceedings. He has two invited journal papers published on IEEE Geoscience and Remote Sensing Magazine. He has two ESI highly cited paper and one ESI hot paper. He has achieved over 10 first authorized Chinese patents. His research has been supported by various funding programs, such as Natural Science Foundation of Jiangsu Province for Distinguished Young Scholars. He has guest edited several special issues in Remote Sensing and etc. He is Senior Member of IEEE now.

 

徐刚，东南大学教授，博士生导师。2016年入职东南大学毫米波全国重点实验室。获得2021年江苏省优青、江苏省科协青年人才、陕西省优博和首届中国电子学会优博提名等。长期从事雷达高分辨成像理论方法研究及工程应用，主要研究方向为合成孔径雷达成像、雷达图像处理与解译、毫米波雷达技术等。主持承担国家自然科学基金等国家级省部级项目10余项。近年来，已在国内外权威期刊及学术会议发表学术论文80余篇，其中，以第一/通信作者在JCR Q1区期刊（IEEE GRSM、TIP、TGRS等）发表论文20余篇。担任在专业领域多个国际国内学术会议分论坛组织者和分会场主席。担任《雷达学报》客座编辑、《电子与信息学报》和《信号处理》青年编委等。

Research Group Introduction

[1] High-resolution radar system design and imaging technology/theory, focusing on novel imaging system design, advanced imaging technology and application

[2] Millimeter wave radar technology, including radar system design, radar signal/imaging processing, array signal processing, target detection and tracking

[3] Statistical machine learning theory and intelligent information extraction and interpretation technology in distributed network, radar imaging, image processing and deep learning.
